Gargantua


Gargantua is introduced as the gravitational centerpiece of the Endurance mission. It's a rotating Kerr black hole of such immense proportions that it anchors several orbiting worlds. Its visual presence is defined by a massive accretion disk of glowing gas, which provides the primary light source for the surrounding planets. During the initial approach, the crew discovers the high-stakes reality of "time dilation" near such a massive object; specifically on Miller's Planet.

As the narrative reaches its climax, the black hole evolves from a scenic obstacle into a desperate laboratory for quantum physics. To provide the necessary velocity for the Endurance to reach Edmunds' Planet, Cooper executes a daring gravitational slingshot maneuver, ultimately ejecting himself into the abyss of the event horizon. Rather than facing immediate "spaghettification"(stretching of matter while entering a blackhole resulting in its destruction), Cooper enters a 5-D Tesseract. This sophisticated construct, placed within the heart of the black hole by future beings, allows him to navigate time as a physical dimension and transmit the essential "singular" data required to save humanity.

Being one of the cental themes of Interstellar, Gargantua here is the centre of fascinating scientific concepts like,

Gravitational Slingshot (The Penrose Process): It is maneuver to save fuel of the spacecraft using the gravity of heavenly body in order to accelerate or decelerate spacecraft. In the movie, due to the lack of fuel they cannot simply head to Edmund's planet through propulsion. So they use Gargantua's gravity to accelerate spacecraft with an accurate trajectory towards Edmund's planet.
