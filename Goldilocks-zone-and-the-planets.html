Goldilocks Zone


In a general sense, the Goldilocks Zone (formally known as the circumstellar habitable zone) is the range of distance around a star where conditions are "just right" for liquid water to exist on a planet's surface. Just like the porridge in the fairy tale, planets too close to their star would see their water boil away, while those too far would find it frozen solid. This "habitable zone" is a primary guide for finding Earth-like life.

In Christopher Nolan's Interstellar, this concept takes a mind-bending turn by centering on the supermassive black hole, Gargantua. Instead of basking in the warm glow of a sun planets receive heat and light from its glowing accretion disk. The team had recieved signals from 3 such planets :

Miller's Planet : This planet was too near Gargantua. Due to it's extreme gravity the water on this planet forms humongous tidal waves. And there comes another mind-blowing concept:


Time Dilation : Time dilation is the magic like phenomenon that slows and speed up the flow of time in certain conditions. It is described in Einstein's special theory of relativity. In the movie time is dilated due to the effect of huge gravitation pull from Gargantua. In space time, time can dilate in two condition:

1. Speed of Light : If we approach near the speed of light, time will be slow for us as compared to others on Earth.
2. Gravitational time dilation : According to Einstein, space-time is a fabric. So, massive bodies bend the space-time curve deep. Due to higher gravitational pull from black hole, space-time curve bends to the extent of ending at a point, making time slower here. 1 hr on Miller's planet cost 7 yrs on earth.

Mann's Planet : On this planet day and night, occur for 67hr each with 80% Earth gravity.It's an icy wasteland.

Edmund's Planet : The only world from the Lazarus Missions that proves truly habitable, is a desert-like planet with a breathable atmosphere and stable terrain. Unlike Miller's Planet, it orbits at a safe distance from Gargantua, meaning it is not subject to extreme gravitational time dilation or lethal tidal forces.
